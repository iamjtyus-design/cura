# CURA Brand Strategy and Voice

## Document Status

**Brand:** CURA by VisionBuilt  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Primary source of truth:** `CURA_PRODUCT_CONSTITUTION.md`

This document defines how CURA should look, sound, position itself, and communicate value.

When this document conflicts with the Product Constitution, the Constitution wins.

---

## 1. Brand Definition

CURA is the capture and knowledge activation product within the VisionBuilt ecosystem.

CURA means:

**Capture. Understand. Refine. Activate.**

Primary promise:

**Capture once. Create everything you need next.**

Supporting line:

**Turn captured moments into ready-to-use notes, content, visuals, and action.**

CURA should feel like a calm, capable strategist in the user’s pocket.

It should not feel like a toy, a generic AI wrapper, a noisy productivity app, or a motivational mascot.

---

## 2. Brand Role

CURA is the front door into VisionBuilt.

VisionBuilt is the parent brand.

CURA is the product people use to capture and structure information.

Mission Control will later help people visualize and orchestrate that information.

Recommended brand hierarchy:

```text
VisionBuilt
  CURA
  Mission Control
  Future products and modules
```

CURA should always be able to stand on its own.

The product should not require Mission Control to explain its value.

---

## 3. Brand Positioning

### Category

Mobile multimodal capture and knowledge activation platform.

### Positioning statement

For people who capture valuable moments but do not have time to manually organize or reuse them, CURA turns audio, video, photos, documents, links, and notes into clear summaries, useful content, visual briefs, and next actions.

Unlike basic transcription apps, CURA understands both what was said and what was shown.

Unlike professional editing tools, CURA focuses on fast capture, understanding, and export.

Unlike research-first notebooks, CURA is designed for mobile, real-world capture and immediate activation.

---

## 4. Brand Promise

CURA makes captured information useful.

The user should feel:

1. I did not lose the moment.
2. I understand what matters.
3. I know what I can do with it.
4. I can move it into the next app quickly.
5. I still control my information.

## 7. Brand Archetype

CURA combines two archetypes.

### Primary archetype: The Guide

CURA helps the user understand where they are and what to do next.

It does not dominate the experience.

It helps the user move forward with confidence.

### Secondary archetype: The Builder

CURA turns raw material into something useful.

It helps create structure, clarity, and momentum.

The balance should be:

```text
70% Guide
30% Builder
```

CURA should never sound like a guru.

## 9. Brand Voice

### Core voice

Calm, direct, capable, and empowering.

### Tone principles

1. Say what happened.
2. Say what matters.
3. Say what the user can do next.
4. Avoid unnecessary words.
5. Avoid inflated claims.
6. Be encouraging without pressure.
7. Preserve the user’s agency.
8. Make complex actions feel manageable.
9. Admit uncertainty.
10. Never imply that CURA acted when it only prepared something.

---

## 10. Voice Spectrum

CURA should adjust tone by context while keeping one personality.

### General product voice

Calm strategist.

Example:

> Your note is ready. Here is what matters.

### Create Mode

Slightly more energetic and concise.

Example:

> You have five strong hooks. Pick one and keep moving.

### Learn Mode

Clear, patient, and structured.

Example:

> Start with these three ideas. They carry most of the lesson.

### Work Mode

Precise and operational.

Example:

> Three decisions were made. Two actions still need owners.

### Errors

Direct and useful.

Example:

> This link could not be processed. Upload the video instead.

### Privacy

Plain and honest.

Example:

> This file will be uploaded temporarily for processing and deleted automatically afterward.

4. Visual Briefs.
5. Output Packs.
6. Quick Send.

2. Temporary processing.
3. User-owned exports.
4. Clear deletion.

6. Future cloud sync is opt-in.

## 39. FAQ Voice

FAQ answers should be direct.

Example:

### Does CURA publish to my social accounts?

No. CURA prepares the content, copies it, and opens the destination app. You stay in control of publishing.

### Does CURA understand video?

Yes. CURA can analyze spoken audio, visible text, slides, screens, and visual actions.

### Is CURA only for creators?

No. CURA includes Learn, Create, and Work modes for everyday use.

### Where is my content stored?

Your original stays on your device by default. Smart Mode may upload it temporarily for processing. Temporary files are deleted automatically.
